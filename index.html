Research & Thessis

Undergraduate Thessis

Impact Analysis of Distributed Generation (DG) on Radial Distribution Networks

Keywords: Distributed Generation, Radial Grids, Voltage Regulation, Protection Coordination, Renewable Energy.

Abstract

This research evaluates the integration of Photovoltaic (PV) and Wind units into traditional radial grids. Using algebraic load flow methods, I modeled five distinct DG injection profiles to assess impacts on voltage stability and protection selectivity—specifically addressing the technical challenges posed by bi-directional current flow in traditional systems.

Grid Architecture

Analyzing the shift from uni-directional power flow to bi-directional active distribution networks.

Source Modeling

Mathematical modeling of PV and Wind injection scenarios to calculate real (P) and reactive (Q) power variables.

Voltage Regulation

Impact of DG placement downstream of Load Tap Changers (LTC) causing voltage to exceed ANSI limits ($\pm5\%$).

Protection Selectivity

Analysis of "Blinding of Protection" where DG fault contribution ($I_2$) desensitizes traditional utility relays.
